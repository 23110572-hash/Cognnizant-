# Cognizant — Strategy, Business Plan and Future Goals

Sources: Cognizant FY2025 earnings release (Feb 4, 2026); Cognizant ESG/Synapse pages; company announcements. Figures as published.

## Core strategy: the "AI builder"
Cognizant positions itself as an "AI builder," building the bridge from AI investment to enterprise value by delivering full-stack, enterprise-grade AI solutions. The strategy combines:
- Proprietary platforms: the Cognizant Neuro AI suite (Neuro AI Engineering, Neuro AI Multi-Agent Accelerator, Neuro IT Operations, Neuro Cybersecurity), plus Flowsource and Skygrade.
- Deep industry/domain expertise across verticals (Health Sciences, Financial Services, Products & Resources, CMT).
- A strengthened partnership ecosystem (Microsoft, Google Cloud, AWS, Anthropic, NVIDIA-class alliances, ServiceNow, Adobe, and 85+ strategic alliances).
- Disciplined M&A to add capability (Belcan in 2024 for digital engineering; 3Cloud in 2026 for Azure/AI).

CEO Ravi Kumar S framed 2025 as the year Cognizant "put our AI builder strategy in motion" and returned to the "winner's circle" (industry-leading growth) two years ahead of the target set at its Investor Day. Speaking at the World Economic Forum in Davos in early 2026, Kumar described the first three years of his tenure as stabilization and the next phase as "bold bets" — including AI, outcome-based pricing, and a possible India IPO. He has noted the company closes four to five deals of over $100 million each quarter.

## Financial guidance and targets (issued Feb 4, 2026)
- 2026 revenue: $22.14–$22.66 billion (4.9%–7.4% growth; 4.0%–6.5% in constant currency).
- 2026 Adjusted Operating Margin: ~15.9%–16.1% (10–30 bps expansion).
- 2026 Adjusted Diluted EPS: $5.56–$5.70 (5%–8% growth).
- Q1 2026 revenue: $5.36–$5.44 billion.
- Stated ambition of sustained margin expansion and top-tier revenue growth, funded by continued investment in AI platforms and talent.

## Capital allocation plan
- Plans to return ~$1.6 billion to shareholders in 2026 (including $1 billion of share repurchases).
- Q1 2026 quarterly dividend raised 6.5% to $0.33 per share.
- ~$1.9 billion remaining under the share repurchase authorization as of Dec 31, 2025.

## Growth engines and recent moves
- Large deals: 28 large deals signed in 2025 (TCV $100M+), with large-deal TCV growth of nearly 50% year-over-year.
- AI partnerships: adopted Anthropic's Claude models; expanded Microsoft partnership for agentic AI/Copilot; partnered with Cognition (Devin AI) for autonomous software engineering; expanded Adobe and added Typeface for agentic marketing.
- Platform launches: Cognizant Resilient IT Operations; AI Lab and Cognizant Moment Studio in Bengaluru; Next-Gen Cyber Defense Center in Bengaluru.
- Capacity: new 8,000-seat campus in Visakhapatnam, India (phased through 2033; first phase ~3,000 associates by early 2029).
- The NextGen cost-optimization/restructuring program concluded at the end of 2024.

## Workforce / talent goals
- Synapse skilling initiative: new goal to upskill two million people by the end of 2030 (the original one-million-by-2026 goal was outpaced).
- Continued focus on AI fluency across the workforce and embedding continuous learning into daily workflows.

## Future-of-work point of view
Cognizant's "New work, new world 2026" research argues AI more often changes the nature of tasks than eliminates entire roles, underpinning its message that AI augments rather than simply replaces workers.
