# Cognizant — Company Profile, Recognition, and Acquisitions

Source: Cognizant newsroom and investor relations; Wikipedia (paraphrased for compliance). Figures cross-checked with Cognizant's FY2025 results (reported Feb 4, 2026).

## At a glance
- Cognizant Technology Solutions Corporation (Nasdaq: CTSH) is an American multinational IT consulting and professional services company headquartered in Teaneck, New Jersey.
- Founded in 1994 in Chennai, India, as an in-house technology unit of Dun & Bradstreet; began serving external clients in 1996 and went public in 1998.
- One of the world's leading professional services companies, organized around industry verticals and technology/operations horizontals.
- FY2025 revenue: $21.1 billion (up 7.0% year-over-year), up from $19.7 billion in FY2024.
- About 350,000–357,600 employees (varies by reporting date through Q1 2026).

## Business and delivery model
- Cognizant pioneered a global delivery model that blends local, onshore client management with large-scale offshore development and delivery centers, emphasizing scalable, cost-effective IT services.
- The model pairs deep industry/domain expertise (vertical units) with technology and process capabilities (horizontal units), now anchored by its "AI builder" strategy and the Neuro AI platform suite.
- A large share of the workforce is based in India (Chennai, Hyderabad, and Bengaluru are the largest centers), with delivery centers across the Americas, Europe, and Asia-Pacific.

## Recognition and awards (2025–2026)
- Fortune 500 — listed for the 16th consecutive year; ranked #216 overall and third in the IT services industry in the 2026 edition.
- Fortune "America's Most Innovative Companies" — recognized for the fourth consecutive year (2026 list; also named in 2025), an honor presented by Fortune and Statista.
- Forbes "World's Best Employers 2025" — included for the third consecutive year.
- Everest Group PEAK Matrix — named a Leader across numerous assessments, including ServiceNow Services 2025, Healthcare Payer Intelligent Operations 2026, plus Banking, Payments, AI/GenAI, Insurance CX, Quality Engineering, and Data and Analytics.
- Named a Leader in the 2025 Gartner Magic Quadrant for Custom Software Development Services.
- Recognized as a Horizon 3 Leader in the HFS Horizons: Generative Enterprise Services 2025 report.
- First IT services provider to achieve ISO/IEC 42001:2023 certification (the AI management system standard).

## Major acquisitions (detailed)
- 3Cloud (announced Nov 13, 2025; completed effective Jan 1, 2026) — one of the largest independent Microsoft Azure services providers and a leader in Azure-dedicated AI enablement; significantly expands Cognizant's Azure portfolio and enterprise-AI transformation capabilities. Its client base spans banking and financial services, healthcare, technology, and consumer sectors.
- Belcan (completed Aug 27, 2024) — acquired for approximately $1.3 billion in cash and stock; an engineering, research and development (ER&D) provider of mission-critical digital engineering for commercial aerospace, defense, space, marine, and industrial sectors, establishing Cognizant's leadership in aerospace & defense.
- Thirdera (announced Dec 13, 2023) — an Elite ServiceNow Partner (advisory, implementation, optimization), making Cognizant one of the world's largest, most credentialed ServiceNow partners.
- Mobica (2023) — automotive, silicon, and IoT software engineering (UK).
- TriZetto (completed 2014) — a landmark ~$2.7 billion acquisition of a healthcare payer/provider software leader, creating an integrated healthcare technology and operations business.
- Earlier digital/cloud/consulting acquisitions (2017–2022) include Servian, New Signature, Collaborative Solutions, Contino, and Softvision.

## Headquarters and listing
- Headquarters: Teaneck, New Jersey, United States.
- Listed on the Nasdaq Global Select Market (ticker CTSH) since its June 1998 IPO; a component of the Nasdaq-100 and the S&P 500.
