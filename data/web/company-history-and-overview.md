# Cognizant — Company History and Overview

Source: https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Cognizant (content paraphrased for compliance with licensing restrictions; figures cross-checked with Cognizant's FY2025 results)

## Snapshot
- Full name: Cognizant Technology Solutions Corporation
- Type: Public company (Nasdaq: CTSH, Class A); component of the Nasdaq-100 and S&P 500
- Industry: Information technology, consulting, outsourcing
- Founded: January 26, 1994, in Chennai, Tamil Nadu, India
- Founder: Kumar Mahadeva (with Srini Raju as co-founding CEO/MD)
- Predecessor: Dun & Bradstreet
- Headquarters: Teaneck, New Jersey, U.S.
- CEO: Ravi Kumar S (Ravi Kumar Singisetti), since January 2023
- FY2025 revenue: ~$21.1 billion; net income ~$2.23 billion; total assets ~$20.7 billion
- Employees: ~350,000+ (≈349,800 as of September 2025)

## History and milestones
- 1994: Established in Chennai as Dun & Bradstreet Satyam Software (DBSS), a 76:24 joint venture between Dun & Bradstreet and Satyam Computers. Began with about 50 employees as Dun & Bradstreet's in-house technology unit.
- 1996: Began pursuing customers beyond Dun & Bradstreet. Dun & Bradstreet spun off several subsidiaries to form Cognizant Corporation.
- 1997: DBSS renamed itself Cognizant Technology Solutions. Dun & Bradstreet bought Satyam's 24% stake.
- 1998: Headquarters moved to the United States; Kumar Mahadeva named CEO. The parent Cognizant Corporation split into IMS Health and Nielsen Media Research; Cognizant became a public subsidiary of IMS Health, which conducted an IPO of Cognizant stock in June 1998 (listed on NASDAQ).
- 2003: IMS Health sold its entire 56% stake. Kumar Mahadeva resigned as CEO and was replaced by Lakshmi Narayanan. The services portfolio expanded into BPO and business consulting.
- 2004: Added to the NASDAQ-100 Index.
- 2006: Francisco D'Souza became CEO. Moved from the S&P 400 to the S&P 500.
- 2011: Became a Fortune 500 company.
- 2014: Acquired healthcare IT services provider TriZetto Corp for $2.7 billion.
- 2019: Brian Humphries became CEO (replacing Francisco D'Souza).
- 2023: Ravi Kumar S (former Infosys president) named CEO, succeeding Brian Humphries.
- 2024: Announced partnership with Microsoft to extend generative AI and Copilot adoption; acquired Belcan (digital engineering).
- 2026: Completed acquisition of 3Cloud (Microsoft Azure services).

## Leadership timeline (CEOs)
Kumar Mahadeva (1998–2003) → Lakshmi Narayanan (2003–2006) → Francisco D'Souza (2006–2019) → Brian Humphries (2019–2023) → Ravi Kumar S (2023–present)

## Operations
- As of recent years, a large share of employees are in India (Chennai, Hyderabad, and Bengaluru are the largest centers). Delivery centers also operate in the UK, Australia, Hungary, Netherlands, Spain, China, Philippines, Canada, Brazil, Argentina, Mexico, and Costa Rica.
- Organized into vertical units (industries such as Banking & Financial Services, Insurance, Healthcare, Manufacturing, Retail) and horizontal units (technology/process areas such as analytics, BPO, and testing). Business consultants across both form the Cognizant Consulting team.

## Notable acquisitions (selected)
- 3Cloud (2026, USA) — Cloud / Microsoft Azure services
- Belcan (2024, USA) — Digital engineering
- Thirdera (2023, USA) — ServiceNow elite partner
- Mobica (2023, UK) — Automotive, silicon, IoT
- TriZetto Corp (2014, USA) — Healthcare payer/provider software (a landmark $2.7B deal)
- Plus a large series of digital, cloud, CRM, and consulting acquisitions through 2019–2022 (e.g., Servian, New Signature, Collaborative Solutions, Contino, Softvision).

## Corporate social responsibility
- Cognizant Foundation (registered 2005 in India) supports access to education and healthcare for underprivileged communities, partnering with NGOs, educational and healthcare institutions, and government agencies.
- "Outreach" is a grassroots employee-volunteer CSR program for skill-building and workforce programs.

## Sponsorships
- Title sponsor of the Aston Martin Formula One team (from 2021).
- Global Partner of the Presidents Cup and title partner of the LPGA Tour's Founders Cup (2021).
- SailGP digital transformation partner (2021).
- Title sponsor of Major League Cricket (from 2024).

## Controversies (for completeness)
Over the years Cognizant has faced various legal and labor-related matters, including: a U.S. Foreign Corrupt Practices Act investigation (2016, related to payments in India) that led to leadership changes; an Indian dividend distribution tax dispute; H-1B visa and wage-related cases in the U.S.; a discrimination lawsuit (a 2024 retrial found against the company); and widely reported poor working conditions for Facebook content moderators at U.S. sites (2019), after which Cognizant announced it would exit parts of the content moderation business in 2020.
